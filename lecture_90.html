Constructor Injection: Behind the Scenes

Mastering how Spring handles dependency management and component lifecycle.

The Spring Processing Workflow

When you start your Spring application, the framework automatically handles the complex task of "wiring" your components together. Here is what happens behind the scenes for your CourseController and Instructor dependency:

  • Component Scanning: Spring detects classes marked with annotations like @Component (in your JavaInstructor implementation) and @RestController.
  • Bean Creation: Spring manages the lifecycle by instantiating your classes, effectively performing new JavaInstructor() on your behalf.
  • Dependency Resolution: Spring recognizes that CourseController depends on an Instructor interface via its constructor.
  • Injection: Spring automatically "injects" the concrete JavaInstructor instance into the CourseController, ensuring your application is fully assembled and ready for use.
